My Projects

Team research project on prototype perovskite solar cells manufactured by NLR (National Lab of the Rockies). Cells were subjected to heat and stress over the course of data collection to simulate real-world use.

Developed a research proposal to analyze the effect of the C60 layer application method on a cell's external quantum efficiency (EQE) degradation along the UV band. Modeled degradation and calculated uncertainty ranges for mean EQE values for each cell. Performed bootstrap resampling of degradation curves within uncertainty ranges. Demonstrated statistically significant differences in model coefficients based on C60 condition, implying association with EQE degradation rate. Communicated results to NLR.

Desktop application intended for use by chemical laboratories to track, search, filter, and generate audits of their inventories. Includes UI customization settings, several concurrent windows, descriptive error handling, and file handling. Created from scratch using JavaFX framework and object-oriented Java principles for my Computer Science II course.

Group lab project for Physics I course focused on maximum precision and accuracy. Designed a precise simple pendulum apparatus from scratch using photogates and motion detectors. Performed detailed uncertainty analysis and derived the theory/simple harmonic motion equations behind the experiment. Analyzed photogate data from differently shaped pendulum weights to determine the optimal setup, modeling the inaccuracy of our equipment. Produced detailed report and acquired six sig-fig experimental value with under 1% discrepancy from theoretical value and under 2% uncertainty.
